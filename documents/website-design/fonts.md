# Fonts : the catalogue and the selection layer

Type is the fastest way a site reads as "designed by a studio" or "generated by AI". The failure mode is reaching for the same handful of "nice" fonts every time (Inter, Poppins, and lately even the refined defaults like Fraunces / Prata / Jost). The fix is a **method**, not a favourite: choose **by role and by brand**, from a wide categorised catalogue, **preferring a famous well-cut face for reliability but deliberately trying a niche one for character**, and **rotating** so no pairing repeats across projects.

Everything here is Google Fonts (portable build). Updated 2026.

## The selection layer (run it, do not reflex)

1. **Decide the two roles.** A **display/voice** face (headlines, the signature) and a **body/UI** face (paragraphs, controls, labels). Sometimes a third **accent** (a mono, or an italic, or a caps face) for prices/labels/eyebrows.
2. **Choose by brand + archetype + dial**, not by habit:
   - luxe / jewelry / fashion : a high-contrast didone or an elegant serif, or a refined light geometric sans. Restraint and contrast.
   - editorial / culture : a text serif + a grotesque; or an expressive display.
   - tech / SaaS : a geometric or neo-grotesque sans; a mono accent.
   - heritage / food : a humanist or old-style serif, warm.
   - brutalist / bold : a heavy grotesque or a display face; a mono.
   Match the cut to the feeling. A terminal mono on a jewelry brand is wrong; a delicate didone on a hardware startup is wrong.
3. **Prefer famous, but try niche.** Pick at least one face that is NOT the obvious default: pair a reliable famous body with a more characterful or niche display (or vice versa). The mix is what stops it looking generated.
4. **Pair by contrast.** Display and body should differ clearly (serif vs sans, high-contrast vs low, condensed vs wide). Two near-identical faces read as a mistake.
5. **Rotate (anti-repeat).** Check the ledger: do not reuse a display face from the last project in the same archetype, and never the exact pairing twice running. Log the pairing on the ledger row.
6. **Rotate the pairing *structure*, not only the families.** The lazy default everyone (and every AI) falls into is "one characterful display serif + one plain sans body", every time, just swapping the names. Break the shape itself: a **single sans superfamily** (weight + size contrast, no second family), a **serif body under a sans display** (inverted), an **all-grotesque** system, a **mono-led** pair, an **expressive sans display**. If recent projects were all elegant-serif-head + plain-sans-body, the next one must not be: the structure of the pairing is part of the fingerprint, and "elegant heading, normal body" on every site is a tell. (A clean single-sans system also reads more institutional / corporate.)
7. **Load only what you use** (variable where possible, Latin subset, the 2 to 4 weights actually used).

## Banned as the primary faces

Defaults and AI tells: `Inter`, `Roboto`, `Arial`, `Helvetica`, `system-ui`, `Open Sans`, `Lato`, `Nunito` (plain), `Poppins`, `Montserrat`. Use `Space Grotesk` only with a real reason (it became its own template). And do not reflex to the "AI-elegant" trio either (`Fraunces`, `Prata`, `Jost`) just because they are pretty: they are fine faces, but only when chosen deliberately and not as the safe pick. A client brand that genuinely owns one of these is the only exception, flagged in the brief.

## The catalogue (famous first, then niche, per category)

**Neo-grotesque / workhorse sans** (body, UI)
- Famous: `DM Sans`, `Hanken Grotesk`, `Manrope`, `Plus Jakarta Sans`, `Figtree`, `Sora`, `Public Sans`.
- Niche / characterful: `Familjen Grotesk`, `Schibsted Grotesk`, `Onest`, `Geist`, `Mona Sans`, `Wix Madefor Text`, `Instrument Sans`, `Hanken`, `Spline Sans`, `Albert Sans`, `Gantari`.

**Geometric sans** (modern, branded)
- Famous: `Outfit`, `Jost`, `Lexend`, `Questrial`.
- Niche: `Gantari`, `Darker Grotesque` (display-leaning), `Hanken Grotesk` (geo-humanist), `Big Shoulders Text`.

**Text serif** (editorial body, warmth)
- Famous: `Newsreader`, `Lora`, `Source Serif 4`, `EB Garamond`, `Spectral`, `Crimson Pro`, `Literata`.
- Niche: `Young Serif` (chunky display-serif), `Petrona`, `Hedvig Letters Serif`, `Fraunces` (variable, SOFT/WONK axes), `Gelasio`, `Bitter`.

**Didone / high-contrast display** (luxe, fashion, jewelry)
- Famous: `Playfair Display`, `Bodoni Moda`, `Cormorant` / `Cormorant Garamond`, `DM Serif Display`, `Abril Fatface`.
- Niche: `Prata`, `Italiana`, `Gilda Display`, `Hedvig Letters`, `Rufina`.

**Elegant / engraved display** (refined headings, caps)
- `Marcellus`, `Forum`, `Cinzel`, `Tenor Sans`, `Cormorant SC`, `Italiana`.

**Expressive / statement display** (dial 3 to 4)
- Famous: `Bricolage Grotesque`, `Syne`, `Anton`, `Bebas Neue`, `Archivo` / `Archivo Expanded` / `Archivo Black`, `Oswald`.
- Niche / loud: `Unbounded`, `Big Shoulders Display`, `Climate Crisis`, `Bungee`, `Rampart One`, `Instrument Serif` (display italic), `Hanken` (heavy).

**Slab serif** (sturdy, editorial-utilitarian)
- `Roboto Slab`, `Zilla Slab`, `Bitter`, `Josefin Slab`, `Aleo`.

**Monospace** (labels, prices, technical accent, never a luxe display)
- Famous: `JetBrains Mono`, `Space Mono`, `IBM Plex Mono`, `DM Mono`.
- Niche: `Martian Mono`, `Fragment Mono`, `Sometype Mono`, `Spline Sans Mono`, `Geist Mono`, `Red Hat Mono`.

This is a working catalogue, not exhaustive: any well-cut Google face that fits the brief and is not on the banned list is fair game. The discipline is the method above. When in doubt, look up the current Typewolf / Fontsource "best of" lists for what is fresh, and prefer a face you have not used recently.
