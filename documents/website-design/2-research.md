# Step 2 : Research (before any code)

No Lavder project starts in the editor. First we look at how the best people in the world solve this exact brief, the way a studio spends the first day pinning references to a wall. This step turns the abstract dial and archetype from Step 1 into a concrete visual direction, and guarantees we are reacting to real, current, excellent work instead of regenerating the average of the internet (which is what "AI slop" is).

## Why it is mandatory

The single biggest reason AI output looks like AI: it is the statistical middle of everything it has seen. A studio escapes the middle by anchoring on specific, recent, excellent references and then reacting to them. We do the same, on purpose, every time.

## What to do

1. **Search the web.** Use `WebSearch` / `WebFetch`. Run several queries that cross the sector with the push level and the medium, for example:
   - `<sector> website design award` , `<sector> branding studio site`
   - `best <archetype> web design 2025/2026` , `<mood> website inspiration`
   - For dial 3 to 4: `editorial website design`, `<sector> awwwards`, `immersive <sector> site`.
2. **Mine the galleries** (these curate the top of the field): Awwwards, Godly, Land-book, Lapa Ninja, SiteInspire, Mobbin (flows), Refero, Httpster, Minimal.gallery. Pull from the ones that match the dial (Godly / Awwwards skew expressive; Land-book / SiteInspire skew refined).
3. **Look sideways, not just at competitors.** A trattoria site can borrow from a fashion lookbook; a law firm from an editorial magazine. Cross-pollination is how a result stops looking like a template of its own category.
4. **Collect 6 to 10 references.** For each, capture the link and, in one line, the one thing worth stealing: a type treatment, a layout move, a color story, a motion idea, a signature element. Save screenshots into the project's `research/` folder if useful.

## What to extract (the read, not just the pretty)

For the strongest references, name concretely:
- **Layout move**: what breaks the grid or carries the page (asymmetry, overlap, a sidebar, an oversized element).
- **Type**: the display face character and how big/loud it goes; the pairing.
- **Color**: the dominant + the accent; is it moody, bright, monochrome, dual-tone.
- **Atmosphere**: how depth is created (light, grain, texture, gradient, photography treatment).
- **Signature**: the one memorable element.
- **Motion**: the one high-impact moment.

## Check ourselves (anti-repetition)

Open [`concept-ledger.md`](./concept-ledger.md) and read the recent rows, especially the same archetype. Write into the brief's `DIVERGE FROM` line what we must NOT repeat: the last neighbor's hero pattern, palette, section-intro device and signature. Step 3 must move away from these.

## Output of Step 2 (the gate)

A short **direction note** appended to the brief, plus the reference links / moodboard:

```
REFERENCES:    <6 to 10 links, each with the one thing to steal>
WHAT WORKS:    <2 to 4 patterns the best references share for this brief>
WHAT TO AVOID: <the category cliches seen repeatedly, and our own ledger neighbors>
STEAL LIST:    <3 to 5 concrete moves we will adapt (layout / type / color / motion / signature)>
```

Gate to pass before Step 3: the direction note exists, it names concrete moves (not vibes), and it explicitly diverges from our recent ledger work. If the research only surfaced generic templates, search harder or wider before continuing. A weak moodboard guarantees a weak result.
