# About Lavder Enterprise

## The company

**Lavder Enterprise Srl** is a holding company founded in Italy and headquartered in Bucharest. It is the legal and strategic umbrella under which every Lavder-owned brand operates.

It does not sell a single service. It builds, owns, and scales products and service companies that share one design language, one voice, one level of craft.

## The relationship

The Enterprise brand is the **parent system**. Everything a sub-brand uses — colors, typography, voice, logo construction, motion — descends from these tokens. A sub-brand can extend, but should not contradict.

Visually, Lavder Enterprise and Lavder share the same identity. The LVDR monogram is the shared mark. The word *Enterprise* appears only where a legal or institutional signal is required (footer of legal pages, company documents, contracts, invoices). In product, marketing, and communication, the mark is simply **Lavder**.

## Positioning adjectives

Three words, in order of weight:

1. **Corporate** — we speak to decision-makers. We show up in rooms where contracts are signed.
2. **Authoritative** — we don't hedge. We write declarative sentences. We show work, not decks of claims.
3. **Modern** — we are not a legacy agency. We use the tools of 2026: AI-native workflows, design systems, component-driven delivery.

## Core promise

*We build brands, products and systems that hold up at scale — technically, visually, and commercially.*

## Relationship to the client

One phrase carries the Lavder positioning more than any other: **partner di fiducia** — *trusted partner*.

Lavder is not positioned as a vendor, an agency-for-hire, or a tool. It is positioned as the team the client brings inside. *"Più che la tua agenzia web, vogliamo essere il tuo partner di fiducia."* Every piece of Lavder communication — copy, sales, proposals, case studies — should reinforce that frame. Vendor language (*deliverable*, *stakeholder*, *scope*) is allowed in operations; in brand-facing communication it is not.

## Languages

- **EN** — primary. Website, brand book, product UI.
- **IT** — local Italian market, lavder.com, legacy clients.
- **RO** — Bucharest operations, Romanian clients.

Copy should be written in EN first, then translated. Never write IT-first and translate to EN — the voice flattens.
